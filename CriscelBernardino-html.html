Cell Organelles and their functions

  1. Nucleus:
    • Controls cell activities.
    • Contains genetic information about the cell (DNA).
    • Carries an essential structure called chromosomes.
  2. Mitochondria:
    • Produces energy in the form of ATP and helps in the transformation of the molecules.
  3. Endoplasmic Reticulum:
    • Transportation system of a eukaryotic cell.
    • Synthesizes proteins (rough ER).
    • Synthesizes lipids (smooth ER).
  4. Golgi Apparatus:
    • Modifies proteins.
    • Sorts and packages proteins and lipids for secretions.
  5. Lysosomes:
    • Helps in digestion.
    • Breaks down waste.
    • Recycles cellular debris.
  6. Ribosomes:
    • Synthesizes proteins.
  7. Chloroplasts:
    • Conducts photosynthesis in plant cells.
  8. Cell Membrane:
    • Provides shape, protects the inner organelles of the cell and acts as a selectively permeable membrane.
    • Regulates entry and exit of substances.
  9. Cell Wall:
    • Provides structure and protection (in plant cells).
  10. Vacuoles:
    • Stores nutrients.
    • Holds waste.
    • Maintains water balance.
    • Provides shape and rigidity to the plants.
